Topic Proposal

Please email me a brief topic proposal for your research essay by Wednesday, March 9.

This proposal should tell me:

  • What your topic is.
  • Why did you pick this topic?
  • Your key terms, i.e., the search terms you are using to find research sources.
  • Your research question(s). (Note: Your research question should change over time, as your ideas evolve. But it’s good to put your initial question in writing.)
  • What “conversation” your research essay will enter. Who talks about this issue, and what are their main claims? What are the enduring questions or debates that surround your topic?
  • What you think your paper will add to the conversation (obviously you don’t know yet, but take a guess).

There is no length or formatting requirement here. However, I will send you a brief comment with suggestions for developing a stronger topic, so it is in your best interest to write as much as you can, as clearly as you can. Since this is a planning activity, it is acceptable to ask questions in this proposal, so that we can start trying to answer them early. This is not an assessment, it’s an opportunity for me to point you in the right direction.
