The Research Paper

This assignment is “simple.” Write a research paper on a topic of your choosing.

The best final draft of this assignment will:

  • Show an attempt to make an original or surprising “argument” in a world where it feels like everything has already been said.
  • Show an attempt to explain to the reader why the topic is significant and deserving of attention.
  • Demonstrate your use of the resources and insights our librarian provides us with.
  • Utilize and articulate a “method” — no matter how simple.
  • Be “cohesive” — even if there are subtopics and tangents, an explanation of how these relate to the main point is provided.
  • Articulate the “conversation” your research is a part of.
  • Cite at least six sources, correctly, in MLA format. Three of these must be “academic.”

There are two auxiliary components to this assignment. You will write an annotated bibliography during your research phase, and you will orally present your findings to the class.

This assignment is worth 15% of your final grade. The final draft will be due Friday, Apr 17 by 11:45pm. You will turn in a 1500-word research paper by email and on Eli Review.
