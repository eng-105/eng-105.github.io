Resources

As they come to my attention, I will be providing online resources to help you with your papers on this page.

Peer Review

  • Feedback and Improvement

    Eli Review’s set of tips for giving great feedback – and why it matters. Has some informative, interesting video content.

Definition Assignment

  • The Purdue Owl

    This is a wonderful resource for all things writing. You’ll need to click around a little to find what you are looking for specifically, but see, especially, the pages on:

    • Style
    • Research and Citation
    • Rhetorical Situations (Features subpages on audience and purpose)
    • Steps for Revising

    But, again, you’ll want to click around and explore the site on the whole, rather than restricting yourself to these particular pages.

  • James, Jasinski, Sourcebook on rhetoric key concepts in contemporary rhetorical studies

    This is an eBook which is available for free through the UB library. It contains good entries on “audience,” “argument,” “genre,” and “style.”

  • Catherine Haar on revision

    For those whose term is “revision,” obviously. This would be a good place for you to start.

Day in the Life at UB

  • The University of Mississippi’s Multimodal Composition Guide

    Contains tutorials and helpful links about editing video, audio, images, websites, etc.

  • UB’s guide to saving and sharing files with OneDrive

    OneDrive is a campus-supplied tool for (among other things) sharing files, videos, etc. without making them available to the public internet.

  • Silverman Library Recording Studios

    UB’s Silverman library actually has recording studios, with editing stations, relevant software, etc. The page says it’s great for everything from recording podcasts to practicing for class presentations.
